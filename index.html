Our Story

Rooted in the Luo word "mich" — a gift.

Mich Scholars Fund was founded to honor Mical, a dedicated educator who has led a life of service in rural Kenya. As a primary school headteacher, she has witnessed firsthand how poverty blocks access to education for countless bright children. Over the years, Mical has quietly paid school fees out of her own pocket, helped families apply for scholarships, and offered mentorship and hope to students in need.

Her inspiration stems from her own experience as a mother. Despite immense financial challenges, she worked tirelessly to educate her children, one of whom completed high school thanks to a scholarship. That act of generosity left a deep mark on her, and she has been paying it forward ever since.

Mich Scholars Fund is now her legacy a formal way to scale her lifelong commitment to fairness, dignity, and education. We believe that every child deserves a chance, regardless of their financial background.

The Problem

Poverty keeps too many children out of the classroom.

In many Kenyan communities, families must pay for uniforms, books, meals, and exam fees — often an impossible burden for struggling households. While public education exists, these hidden costs create a barrier that locks children out of school.

Girls are disproportionately affected, facing early marriage, child labor, and inconsistent attendance due to lack of sanitary supplies. The result is lost potential and deepening inequality.

At a glance

  • Millions of Kenyan children miss school due to poverty
  • Uniforms, books & exam fees are often unaffordable
  • Girls face the highest dropout rates
  • Lost education = lost opportunity for entire communities
